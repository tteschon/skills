# ChompSaw design constraints

The ChompSaw (chompshop.com) is a kid-safe powered cardboard cutter. Every
project part must be designed with these physical realities in mind.

## Material
- Corrugated cardboard **3 mm or thinner** only. Thicker board won't fit the
  cutting head. Standard shipping-box cardboard works.
- No tape, glue, paint, or stickers on cardboard that will pass through the
  saw. Sticky residue gums up the mechanism. All cutting happens FIRST,
  assembly and decoration afterward.

## How it cuts
- The user pushes cardboard **forward, away from themselves**, into the
  cutting head. It will not cut sideways.
- **Curves are made by rotating the cardboard** while pushing forward. Gentle,
  continuous curves are easy; very tight curves (radius under ~10 mm) are hard
  to steer cleanly. Prefer radii of 10 mm or more.
- **Interior cutouts** (donut holes, windows, slots) need a starter hole made
  with the Hole Punch accessory. The cardboard is then fed onto the saw from
  that hole. Every enclosed interior cut in a pattern needs a punch mark.
- Sharp inside corners are approximated by cutting into the corner from two
  directions, or by rounding them. Round interior corners when it doesn't
  hurt the design - they're easier and stronger.

## Companion tools (design for them when helpful)
- **Hole Punch**: clean small holes - use for axles, rubber-band anchors,
  string holes, and starter holes for interior cuts.
- **Scoring Tool**: creases cardboard for clean folds. Any fold line in a
  pattern should be drawn dashed and labeled "score and fold - do not cut".
- **Workstation**: holds the saw; no design implications.

## Structural tricks that work well in 3 mm cardboard
- **Slot joints**: two parts with mating slots (slot width = cardboard
  thickness, ~3-3.5 mm) press together without glue.
- **Tabs + glue**: fold tabs on scored lines, glue to neighboring faces.
- **Lamination**: glue 2-3 identical cutouts together for stiff parts
  (wheels, gears, axle supports).
- **Tubes**: repurpose paper-towel / wrapping-paper tubes for cylinders, or
  roll thin cardboard around a form. Common paper-towel tube: ~45 mm outer
  diameter. Toilet-paper tube: ~42 mm.

## Audience and safety
This is a children's maker tool. Designs must stay kid-safe:
- Launcher-type projects (catapults, cannons, planes) use rubber-band power
  at most, and launch only soft projectiles (pom-poms, cotton balls, paper).
  Include a "never aim at faces/people/pets" note in the guide.
- No sharp points, no heavy swinging parts, no real blades, nothing combustive.
- Flag hot glue steps as adult-supervised.
